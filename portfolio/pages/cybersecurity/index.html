Cybersecurity

CIS 480 - Introduction to Network Security

  CIS 480 offered an in-depth exploration of network security, providing a comprehensive understanding of both theoretical and practical aspects of protecting information systems. Covered was a broad spectrum of topics, including TCP/IP fundamentals, virtualization, network traffic analysis, routing, cryptography, and wireless security. The course's practical approach, which involved the design and management of firewalls, the implementation of VPNs, and strategies to secure web resources, has equipped me with a robust set of skills to identify vulnerabilities and strengthen network defenses effectively.

  • Network Security: Understanding the landscape of network threats and defense mechanisms enables me to contribute to safeguarding an organization's data and infrastructure.
  • Cryptography & Data Protection: Familiarity with cryptographic standards and cryptanalysis methods allows me to ensure the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive business information.
  • Wireless Security: With the rise of mobile and remote work, securing wireless networks is critical, and I am now capable of implementing robust security measures.
  • Intrusion Detection & Prevention: The ability to set up and manage IDS/IPS systems means I can swiftly detect and mitigate potential breaches.
  • Firewall & VPN Management: Designing and maintaining firewalls, along with configuring VPNs, ensures secure and private communications necessary for business operations.

CIS 481 - Introduction to Information Security

 CIS 481 provided a thorough grounding in the fundamentals of cybersecurity, covering an array of essential topics such as confidentiality, integrity, availability, various security models, and the complex landscape of cyber threats and defenses. The course's comprehensive approach spanned from the basics of authentication models and intrusion detection to the nuances of legal and ethical considerations in information security. This was not just theoretical; it involved practical applications, case studies, and simulations that mirrored real-world scenarios.

  • Comprehensive Security Understanding: Knowledge of various security threats and the corresponding defenses ensures I can contribute to safeguarding sensitive company data and infrastructure.
  • Policy and Governance: Ability to formulate and enforce security policies, and understand the role of management in cybersecurity, aligns with corporate governance and regulatory compliance needs.
  • Risk Management and Analysis: Skills in identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks are crucial for maintaining an organization's security posture.
  • Incident Response and Recovery: Preparedness for handling security incidents and implementing effective recovery strategies is vital for business continuity.
  • Physical Security Measures: Understanding the integration of cybersecurity with physical security measures is essential for comprehensive protection strategies.

CIS 483 - Introduction to Database Security

 CIS 483 provided an extensive education in the realm of database security, focusing primarily on SQL Server and Windows security. This course was aided in understanding the complexities of protecting data within database management systems. It covered a range of topics, from the fundamentals of database security to more advanced concepts like database penetration testing, data encryption, and secure database architecture. Practical exercises in user management, security policy integration, and ownership chaining further solidified this understanding. The course's emphasis on both theoretical knowledge and practical application makes it highly relevant in today's data-driven business environment.

  • Database Security Fundamentals: Understanding the core principles of database security helps in safeguarding sensitive business data against breaches and unauthorized access.
  • Access Control Mechanisms: Skills in managing access controls ensure that data is accessible only to authorized personnel, maintaining data confidentiality and integrity.
  • Secure Database Architecture Development: Ability to design and manage secure databases aligns with the need for robust data infrastructure in businesses.
  • Data Encryption Techniques: Understanding data encryption offers ways to protect data at rest, a crucial aspect of data security.
  • Vulnerability Assessment: Learning about the vulnerabilities of systems like SQL Server and Oracle prepares me to identify and address potential security gaps.

CIS 484 - Computer Forensics

 CIS 484 provided a comprehensive understanding of computer forensics, a field that is increasingly important in the digital age. This course covered the entire spectrum of forensic processes, from the initial collection and preservation of digital evidence to its examination, analysis, and eventual presentation in legal contexts. A significant focus was on using technology tools to analyze files, with a keen eye on the implications of privacy, ethics, and policy in forensic activities. The hands-on approach of the course, coupled with detailed theoretical instruction, offered a deep dive into various forensic acquisition methodologies such as static, live, targeted, and remote acquisitions.

  • Evidence Collection and Preservation: Understanding the proper techniques for collecting and preserving digital evidence is crucial in a corporate environment, especially when dealing with data breaches or legal disputes.
  • Digital Evidence Analysis: Proficiency in analyzing core Windows artifacts like Event Logs, Registry, Browsing History, and more, enables the extraction of crucial information from corporate systems.
  • Legal and Ethical Considerations: Awareness of legal and ethical issues surrounding digital forensics ensures compliance and responsible handling of sensitive data.
  • Forensic Reporting: Skills in preparing detailed forensic reports are vital for communicating findings to non-technical stakeholders, including legal teams and corporate management.
  • Data Recovery: Ability to recover deleted files can be essential in various business scenarios, from accidental deletion to investigating malicious activities.
