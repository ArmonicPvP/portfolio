Database Management

CIS 305 - Data Analysis for Decision-Making

 CIS 305 provided me with a comprehensive understanding of the connection between information systems and information technology, particularly in how they support and facilitate business processes. The course was focused on developing a broad literacy in information systems, a crucial component for all business majors. One of the key strengths of this course was its emphasis on practical applications of technology in business analysis and data management.

  • Relational Database Management: Proficiency in creating, editing, and exploring objects in a relational database management system (RDBMS), which is crucial for managing and organizing business data effectively.
  • Data Validation: Ability to build and apply data validation rules in database and spreadsheet applications, ensuring the accuracy and integrity of business data.
  • Data Analysis and Summarization: Skills in analyzing and summarizing vast amounts of data using techniques like wildcards, Boolean logic, and various functions. This competency is essential for business analysts and knowledge workers who need to extract meaningful insights from data.
  • Decision-Support Tools: Familiarity with decision-support tools within database and spreadsheet applications, enabling me to contribute to data-driven decision-making processes.
  • Data Presentation: Ability to visually present raw and summarized data effectively using embedded presentation tools in database and spreadsheet applications. This skill is key in communicating insights clearly and concisely to stakeholders.

CIS 310 - Database Design

 CIS 310 was a comprehensive course that provided a solid foundation in the design and implementation of database systems, with a particular focus on the relational database model. The course was structured to offer both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, with extensive hands-on experience in database design, implementation, and usage. Key aspects included in-depth coverage of entity-relationship (E-R) modeling, normalization, and SQL, alongside an introduction to database administration and current topics like big data. The use of SQL server as the course software further enriched my practical experience in working with databases.

  • Relational Database Design and Implementation: Understanding the fundamentals of relational databases is crucial for creating efficient and scalable data storage solutions in a business.
  • Entity-Relationship Modeling: Proficiency in E-R modeling aids in designing databases that accurately reflect business processes and data requirements.
  • SQL Proficiency: Mastery of SQL, the standard language for database management, allows for effective data manipulation and retrieval, essential for data-driven decision-making in businesses.
  • Database Normalization: Skills in database normalization ensure the minimization of data redundancy and optimization of database performance.
  • Database Administration Fundamentals: Introduction to database administration prepares me for managing and maintaining database systems, a key responsibility in many businesses.
  • Big Data Awareness: Exposure to current topics like big data provides insight into the handling and analysis of large and complex data sets, increasingly important in modern business environments.
